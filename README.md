# Dual-channel_screening_of_thrombin_inhibitors

This repository contains data related to the article entitled "Dual-Channel Virtual Screening Identifies a Novel 1,2,4-Triazole Thrombin Inhibitor Hit from Natural Products".

ABSTRACT: Thrombin is a key protease in the coagulation cascade, and its abnormally elevated activity is closely associated with thrombotic diseases such as myocardial infarction and stroke. Existing anticoagulants are limited by a high bleeding risk and narrow therapeutic windows, creating an urgent need for novel inhibitors that are both efficient and safe. To address this, we established a dual-channel virtual screening workflow integrating machine learning classification with multi‑program consensus docking. Screening of 156,828 natural products yielded 91 high-priority candidates, which were further filtered by drug‑likeness, ADME/T properties, and short molecular dynamics prescreening. Ten compounds were selected for enzymatic evaluation, and one hit, 6855‑0119, displayed measurable thrombin inhibition with an IC50 of 57.67 μM. This compound features a previously underexplored 1,2,4‑triazole scaffold and revealed a stable multi-subsite binding mode involving S1 pocket anchoring, π-π interactions with Trp60 and Trp215, and persistent hydrogen bonding with catalytic residues. These results demonstrate the feasibility of the dual-channel strategy for identifying novel scaffolds and highlight 6855-0119 as an initial proof-of-concept starting point for further optimization.
